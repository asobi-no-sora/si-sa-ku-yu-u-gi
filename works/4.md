#  Ⅳ
*Works*

### static

the set does not sort what arrives.
a compressor in the next room,
the sun's hiss, the galaxy's hiss,
and mixed with the rest,
light that has been moving
since before there was a hand
to someday find this dial —
no louder for its age,
no faster for the distance,
taking the same place in the noise
as everything else with nowhere left to go.

a hand finds the channel it was looking for.
the snow is gone — not used up,
only no longer where the looking is.
it goes on arriving in the next room, and the next —
in static no one has tuned to yet.


### vacancy

the hind half of the body has no armor of its own —
only the coiled give of it, built to grip a house it never grew.
something elsewhere has to stop needing its shell first:
a beak, a fracture, or nothing worth naming —
and what is left behind
is the right size for no one yet.

the first to arrive goes in partway,
comes back out too big to close the door behind it,
and stays close anyway —
a line is already sorting itself by size.

no one moves until the largest does.
then no one stops moving —
shell into shell into shell,
and then there is no line left to call one.

the smallest is last.
it finishes the chain wearing a room
that, a moment ago,
fit somebody it will never meet.


### nucleus

before the face is finished,
a few cells arrive first,
dead center.

once growth is finished,
they shed their own nucleus,
their mitochondria,
everything with a name and a function —
not dying. emptying.

what is left is only protein,
packed close enough to be clear,
never replaced.

more cells follow, every year,
further out, the same way —
and each ring buries the one before it.

the center keeps the day it was made,
exactly as it arrived —
so precisely that years later,
a carbon count
can still name the year.


### silver

first the gut stops asking.
there will be nothing to digest
where it is going.

the eyes outgrow the sockets that held them,
sized for a dark not yet seen —
back gone black, belly taking the color
of what it is about to vanish into.

one autumn night, at a temperature it was waiting for,
it leaves the river — twenty years in it, sometimes thirty —
and does not eat again — six thousand kilometers
on fat banked years before.

a century of looking
has never found the egg,
never found one of them, arrived.

two years on, something else comes in instead,
leaf-shaped, transparent,
carried by a current that does the choosing for it —
not knowing there was one before it,
not knowing it will be one too.
